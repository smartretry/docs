title: General FAQ
description: Common questions about SmartRetry, how it works, and what to expect.
---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is SmartRetry?">
    **SmartRetry** is an intelligent payment optimization engine that automatically retries declined transactions through the most suitable payment routes. It analyzes decline reasons, acquirer performance, and 3DS responses in real time to increase your overall approval rate and reduce lost revenue.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who is SmartRetry designed for?">
    SmartRetry is designed for **merchants, PSPs, and payment facilitators** who process online transactions and want to improve approval ratios without changing their infrastructure. It's especially valuable for high-volume industries such as subscriptions, gaming, digital goods, and fintech.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does SmartRetry work?">
    SmartRetry connects to your payment flow via a simple API, SDK, or plugin. When a transaction fails, it automatically analyzes the decline reason and applies a retry strategy — choosing the best **acquirer, 3DS setting, currency, or card parameter** to maximize approval chances. Results are visible instantly in your dashboard or API logs.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is SmartRetry a processor?">
    SmartRetry is **not a processor**. It's a **layer on top of your existing payment providers** that enhances approval rates through intelligent retry and routing logic. You keep using your PSPs and acquirers — SmartRetry just helps you get more approvals with them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What makes SmartRetry different from a regular routing system?">
    Traditional routing systems send payments to a fixed acquirer based on static rules. SmartRetry uses **dynamic routing and prescriptive intelligence** — adapting decisions in real time based on live acquirer data, MCCs, ECI levels, and issuer behavior. It doesn't just reprocess transactions — it **learns** from every decline.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can SmartRetry work globally?">
    Yes. SmartRetry supports **global acquirers, regional PSPs, and multi-currency environments**, enabling you to optimize approvals across different markets. It also adjusts logic based on local issuer behaviors, MCC patterns, and 3DS regulations per region.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How secure is SmartRetry?">
    SmartRetry was built with security and privacy at its core. All data is **encrypted in transit and at rest**, and sensitive payment details are never stored on SmartRetry's servers. Access to systems is tightly controlled through **role-based permissions** and multi-factor authentication, ensuring only authorized personnel can reach operational data. The platform undergoes **regular security reviews and penetration testing** to maintain the highest protection standards.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How quickly can SmartRetry be implemented?">
    Integration can take as little as one to two days. You can connect via REST API, SDK, or pre-built connectors for popular PSPs. SmartRetry's technical team also provides sandbox access for testing before you go live.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What kind of results can I expect?">
    On average, SmartRetry users experience a **5–25% improvement in approval rates**, depending on transaction type, region, and acquirer mix. The system continuously learns and optimizes, so performance improves over time as more data is collected.

    <Tip>Results can be immediate for many optimization tools within the SmartRetry Arsenal. Optimal performance is typically reached after about one month of live processing.</Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the SmartRetry Arsenal?">
    The **SmartRetry Arsenal** is SmartRetry's collection of proprietary optimization strategies designed to improve transaction approval rates and reduce declines. It includes dozens of advanced methods — from routing logic and retry sequencing to 3DS tuning and acquirer selection — many of which are publicly discussed on the SmartRetry blog, while others remain exclusive to the internal system.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does SmartRetry use machine learning or fixed rules?">
    SmartRetry combines **machine learning models** with **rule-based logic**. This hybrid approach ensures that it continuously learns from your transaction history while maintaining predictable, transparent behavior where you need it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does SmartRetry work automatically or require manual setup?">
    SmartRetry operates **fully automatically** once integrated, but you can also define **custom retry rules** or adjust parameters like preferred acquirers, currencies, or ECI levels. You remain in control — SmartRetry amplifies your approval results intelligently.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does SmartRetry include analytics or dashboards?">
    Yes. SmartRetry offers **real-time analytics and reporting** showing approval rates, retry outcomes, acquirer performance, and 3DS success rates. The data is accessible through the dashboard, helping you measure impact and identify optimization opportunities.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can SmartRetry handle recurring payments or subscriptions?">
    Yes. SmartRetry includes **specialized logic** for recurring transactions — adjusting timing, acquirer routing, and authentication behavior to minimize declines on subscription renewals or repeat charges.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if I have only one payment provider?">
    Even with a single acquirer, SmartRetry can still apply **advanced retry logic** — including 3DS optimization, data enrichment, and issuer-side adaptations. However, using **multiple acquirers** significantly improves flexibility and approval performance.

    <Note>SmartRetry can recommend when to add or change acquirers based on performance data it collects over time.</Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does SmartRetry manage multiple acquirers?">
    SmartRetry is built for **multi-acquirer environments**. It continuously monitors performance, latency, and approval trends for each acquirer, and routes or retries transactions accordingly. This ensures you're always using the acquirer most likely to approve a given transaction — based on region, card type, MCC, or 3DS result.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

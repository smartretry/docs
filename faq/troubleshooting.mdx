---
title: Troubleshooting
description: Solutions to common issues when using SmartRetry.
---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why are some retries not triggered?">
    In most cases, retries aren't triggered because the **decline reason** doesn't meet the logic criteria for a retry. For example, hard declines like _"stolen card"_ or _"do not honor"_ may be excluded. SmartRetry only retries transactions that have a **realistic chance of succeeding** with another acquirer or configuration, to avoid unnecessary processing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why do I see a difference between SmartRetry metrics and my PSP or acquirer reports?">
    Small differences are normal — SmartRetry tracks **each retry attempt individually**, while most PSPs display only the **final transaction result**. The SmartRetry dashboard is built for transparency, showing every retry, reason code, and acquirer decision in detail.

    <Note>If you notice major inconsistencies, SmartRetry's team can help you reconcile the data.</Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How can I verify that SmartRetry is active and working properly?">
    You can confirm SmartRetry's activity by viewing **retry events** in your dashboard or logs. Each retried transaction includes a reference to the SmartRetry process, with timestamps and result details.

    If you're not seeing retries triggered as expected, contact **support@smartretry.com** or reach out via the Discord channel and the team will review your integration.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What should I do if approval rates haven't improved yet?">
    If your approval rates haven't improved after the first few days, don't worry — SmartRetry's optimization is **progressive**. Performance improves as the system gathers more transaction data and learns your acquirers' behavior.

    <Tip>Contact your account manager to review your setup, acquirer mix, or retry strategy for faster results.</Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if my API connection fails?">
    If your API temporarily fails or doesn't respond, you can monitor the system's current health at any time via the **status page** at [status.smartretry.com](https://status.smartretry.com/).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can SmartRetry be paused or disabled temporarily?">
    Yes — if you want to pause SmartRetry, you can either contact your **account manager** or simply **stop sending transactions** to the API. When no new transactions are sent, SmartRetry automatically remains idle, and you'll only be billed for previously processed transactions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does the DECLINED status mean vs. a failed retry?">
    A **DECLINED** status is the final state of a transaction — it means all retry attempts have been exhausted and no acquirer approved the payment. This is distinct from a **failed retry**, which is an individual unsuccessful attempt within the retry sequence. SmartRetry may make several retry attempts before a transaction reaches the terminal DECLINED state.

    <Info>Check the retry event log in your dashboard to see the full sequence of attempts and the reason codes returned at each step.</Info>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I check the status of a specific transaction?">
    Use the status endpoint to retrieve the current state of a transaction:

    ```http
    GET /v1/payments/status/{terminal_id}/{transaction_id}
    ```

    Replace `{terminal_id}` and `{transaction_id}` with the values for the transaction you want to look up. The response includes the current status, retry history, and the last acquirer decision.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My transaction shows PROCESSING — is it complete?">
    No. A **PROCESSING** status means the transaction is currently in-flight — SmartRetry is actively working through the retry sequence. The transaction has not yet reached a final state.

    <Note>Poll the `GET /v1/payments/status/{terminal_id}/{transaction_id}` endpoint until the status changes to either `APPROVED` or `DECLINED` before treating the transaction as complete.</Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
